OUR WOMEN, OUR FUTURE.

Welcome to WoMedical! We are a youth-led organization dedicated to educating and advocating for women’s health, including topics such as menstrual health, period poverty, and bodily autonomy.

Our Mission

WoMedical is dedicated to supporting economically and socially disadvantaged women through proper health education and feminine care product distribution in NYC’s most impoverished areas. Our ultimate goal is to alleviate pressing struggles that women face and foster a safe, supportive community.

Women's Issues

Feminine Care Product Distribution

We acquire funding from sponsors in order to distribute menstrual products and contraceptives to disadvantaged young women in need.

Periods/Safe Sex Education

We invite speakers to educate girls on health topics including but not limited to periods and safe sex.

Safe Affinity Spaces

We collaborate with community centers to host educational seminars and safe affinity spaces for female students, covering topics ranging from teenage mental health to gynecology.

Shelters

Tillary Street Women's Shelter ▼

The Tillary Street Women's Shelter, located in Downtown Brooklyn, is a 200-bed city-funded shelter for women with mental illness and co-morbid substance abuse disorders. The shelter provides temporary housing, case management, housing referral and (more) placement services, and on-site medical and mental health services.

2 / 10
❮ ❯
The Bowery Mission - Flagship Campus ▼

The Bowery Mission exists to promote the flourishing of New Yorkers overcoming homelessness and marginalization by providing compassionate services and a transformative community. In 2022, they provided 258,000 meals, 56,000 nights of shelter, and 21,000 articles of clothing.

3 / 10
❮ ❯
The Dwelling Place Women's ▼

The Dwelling Place Women’s served as a transitional residence for homeless women in Manhattan. Since 1977, they’ve provided shelter, sustenance, and support to thousands of women, many of them being survivors of abuse.

4 / 10
❮ ❯
The Kensington Family Shelter ▼

The Kensington is a temporary family homeless shelter in Brooklyn with a capacity for 64 families. The staff provides emergency food, clothing, carfare and supportive services.

5 / 10
❮ ❯
Lenox Hill Women's Health Shelter ▼

The Neighborhood House provides a comprehensive network of programs and services to homeless and formerly homeless women to help them to gain independence and stability. Specifically with the Lenox Hill Neighborhood House’s Women’s Mental Health Shelter, it serves as a 24-hour program that addresses the critical issues facing homeless women age 45-and-over living with severe and persistent mental illness.

6 / 10
❮ ❯
Chinese-American Planning Council Queens Community Center ▼

CPC Queens serves over 500 individuals daily and is the largest community based organization serving the Chinese community in Queens. Services include comprehensive youth services, family support, and home and community based services to individuals with developmental disabilities and their families.

7 / 10
❮ ❯
KYOFUKAI - Japan Christian Women’s Organization ▼

KYOFUKAI has worked for women’s basic human rights, such as to end legalized prostitution, gain the right to vote etc. and at the same time, to create laws preventing minors from abusing alcohol, tobacco and drugs. Their long-sought Prostitution Prevention Law was successfully passed in 1956, and they are now working toward the new laws for the 21st century, while continuing their women’s welfare services.

8 / 10
❮ ❯
PROJECT RENEWAL NEW PROVIDENCE WOMEN’S SHELTER ▼

Project Renewal’s mission is to end the cycle of homelessness by empowering individuals and families to renew their lives with health, homes, and jobs. Their nonprofit organization helps homeless and low-income men and women who often have a drug addiction or suffer a mental illness.

9 / 10
❮ ❯
KOREAN UNWED MOTHERS’ FAMILIES ASSOCIATION ▼

Founded in 2009 in Seoul, KUMFA advocates for the rights of single mothers and their children in Korea. Its goal is to enable Korean women to have sufficient resources and support to keep their babies and thrive.

10 / 10
❮ ❯
George Daly House ▼

Located on Manhattan’s Lower East Side, George Daly House provides social services and a short-term housing alternative to the City’s shelters. Housed in some 88 single and double-occupancy rooms, men and women residents benefit from a variety of individual and group services. Assistance ranges from personal finance classes, resumé workshops and dealing with landlords, to cooking instruction and improving self-esteem.

Companies

❮ ❯
Aunt Flow ▼

Aunt Flow is a female-founded company that is committed to ensuring that everyone has access to menstrual products and strives to create a gender inclusive community. They stock public restrooms with freely accessible pads and tampons that are made of organic cotton.

2 / 2
❮ ❯
Knix ▼

KNIX is a female-founded company focused on designing products for women that are comfortable and innovative. They are redesigning clothes and menstrual products that feel good and lightweight on your body in sustainable ways. Knix's Life After Birth project and Black Women's Health Imperative create a community to help people through their postpartum journeys.

The branch offers innovative programming, including a monthly book discussion group, a preschool program and an annual family concert sponsored by Carnegie Hall. In addition, the Dyker Singers, a community-based choral group, rehearses at the library once a week and always welcomes new members.
